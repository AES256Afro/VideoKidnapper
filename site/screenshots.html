A visual tour

Every screenshot is the real app, straight from the window.

The whole flow in ten seconds

Load a clip, drag the trim handles, drop a caption on it, export a GIF. That's the core loop VideoKidnapper is built around.

One tab: load, trim, caption, export

Open a file, record your screen, or paste a link, then work on it right there. Thumbnail strip, waveform, dual-handle range slider, queued cuts, and a caption with outline/shadow/bold that renders on the preview exactly as it exports.

Captions that follow the action

Pin a caption to a moving subject and it tracks them across the frame, like the “click for more” memes. Here one caption follows the shape through three moments of the same clip. Drag to set the path by hand, or click ⚡ Auto-track and let OpenCV follow the subject for you. The preview matches the export the whole way.

Kidnap from a link, or a whole batch

Paste a link (or Ctrl+V from anywhere) and the platform lights up: YouTube, Instagram, X, Reddit, Bluesky, Facebook, and 1,000+ more. Browser-cookie support handles private videos; the batch queue grabs a list in order and drops any one straight into the editor.

Start here: every way to load a video

The empty state lays it out: click the preview, drag a file in, open from disk, record your screen, or paste a link. Import an .srt or auto-caption speech with Whisper.

History: your recent exports

Format, quality preset, timestamp, and size for every export, with one-click Open and Reveal-in-folder.

Setup: prerequisites handle themselves

Missing something? Setup installs it for you with a live console showing exactly what's happening, then drops you into the app. FFmpeg comes bundled in the Store and Linux builds.
